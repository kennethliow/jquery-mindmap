• Mindmap Blog Comments

    Using an interactive mind map for blog post comments instead of the currently ubiquitous linear method.

    • Mindmap vs. Linear

      Linear blog post comments don't represent input from people well

      • Permit representation of complex issues
      • Document all different angles to an argument
      • Allow people to follow different threads along different lines
      • Allow the editing and subsuming collections of arguments into new nodes

    • Interactive

      Interactive means your map changes as people make changes to the map

    • Ultimate Crowdsourcing: Crowdstreaming

      Don't just rely on the crowd to provide content—rely on the crowd to give you content and then to give structure and meaning to that content.

    • Attractive

      Needs to be fun and attractive, i.e., animated if possible

    • Useful

      Interactive mindmap blog comments are much more useful

      • No Spam

        Ok, it's not that there won't be spam, it's that your crowd will be able to classify it as such and thus get it out of the way.

      • Temperature overlays

        A way of rating your agreement with the handling of a complex subject can be done on a node-by-node (or branch by branch) basis, creating a temperature map with shades of green (agree) and red (disagree) and white (neutral). Anybody can easily sign off in a complex way to a complex handling of a complex issue, because you can now both "agree" and "disagree" in a nuanced way.

      • Handle once

        Instead of countless repetitions of the handling of the same socio-political issues over and over again in the public dialogue using linear communication, change the focus of communication for complex issues. One mind map will suffice to get all the viewpoints in, then it is "done" except for tweaks from new people and updates to real life changes.

        Then what do you do with the map? You use it to solve problems!
